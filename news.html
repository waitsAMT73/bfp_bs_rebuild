High Risk Groups

April 3, 2021

The HSE has written to General Practice late this week asking for our assistance in vaccinating those patients aged 18-69 in the 'Very High Risk Group' (Group 4) and the 'High Risk Group' (Group 7) according to the Vaccination Allocation Strategy.

Please click on this link for further details regarding Group 4 and Group 7.

If you think you are eligible for vaccination according to the conditions set out in Group 4 and 7, please click on the MAKE ENQUIRY button below and fill in your details on the form. A member of the team will contact you to inform you of your eligibility.

The HSE will continue to roll out vaccinations for the rest of the population through mass vaccination centres in the coming weeks to months. Therefore we are only offering vaccinations according to Group 4 and Group 7. Our over 70s vaccination programme continues unaffected.

Note: Weight and Height are included as patients are eligible for vaccination if they have a Body Mass Index greater than 35.

Calculate your own BMI using this link: https://www.safefood.net/bmi-calculator

Covid-19 Vaccinations

February 9, 2021

In accordance with the National Covid19 Vaccination Strategy, we are planning our vaccination clinics for our over 70s patient group beginning with patients over 85. We expect to start receiving supply of Covid 19 vaccines from middle to late February.

We will only receive vaccines for those who are registered with the practice and have either an Over 70s Doctor Visit Card or GMS Medical Card.

If you are above 70 years old and a patient of the practice, it is important to ensure you are registered with the Over 70s Visit Card Scheme or the GMS Medical Card scheme.

If you are not registered for an Over 70s Doctor Visit Card, please click here and complete the online application.

Please note : We expect to receive vaccine doses in small batches over a number of weeks. We will therefore contact you directly to book your vaccination appointment when vaccines become available.
